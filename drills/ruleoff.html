Rule off the schedule

Alt H B lays borders where you point — top, bottom, outside, or a THICK box (Alt H B T) around the headline.
Add the borders the schedule is missing and pull the EBITDA line through. Accounting standard: a line under the headers, a line above every subtotal, EBITDA pulled across, bolded and ruled, and a box around the headline figure. The sections and the headline year change every run.

The shortcuts in this drill
Alt walks: tap Alt, release, then the letters in sequence — each key steps one ribbon menu. On a Mac, tap ⌥ and use the same letters (KeyTips, Excel 2024+).
Alt h b o⌥ h b o on macBottom border
Bottom double border closes a finished schedule — the page’s way of saying done.
Alt h b p⌥ h b p on macTop border
Ctrl+R⌘R on macFill right from the cell to the left
Same as fill down, but across — one quarter’s formula becomes the whole year in two keystrokes.
Ctrl+B⌘B on macBold
Bold marks structure: titles, total rows, anything the reviewer’s eye should land on first.
Alt h b t⌥ h b t on macThick box border
Top border above a total row — the accounting ruling that says “this line sums the ones above.”
The optimal line
the par-setting sequence, straight through:
headers B2:E2 alt h b o · rule ABOVE both subtotals B6:E6 and B9:E9 alt h b p · EBITDA B10 = revenue total + cost total, fill right ctrl+r, ctrl+b, alt h b p · box the headline C10 alt h b t (thick box)
