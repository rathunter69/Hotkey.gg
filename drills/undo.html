Undo is a tool

Ctrl+Z rewinds your exact steps — delete, check, rewind if needed, then delete the right thing.
Clear the wrong block and Ctrl+Z brings every cell back exactly as it was. The first note says to clear a scratch block; the second note says that block actually feeds Q4 and the other one was the junk. Undo the mistake, clear the right block, then format the remaining rows.
train this drill → par 10s · optimal 5 keys · keyboard only · free
The shortcuts in this drill
Ctrl+B⌘B on macBold
Bold marks structure: titles, total rows, anything the reviewer’s eye should land on first.
Ctrl+I⌘I on macItalic
Italics are the convention for memos, footnotes, and anything explanatory rather than computed.
Ctrl+Z⌘Z on macUndo
Delete big, undo, keep what deserved to stay — the undo stack is deep, and working fearlessly is faster than working carefully.
