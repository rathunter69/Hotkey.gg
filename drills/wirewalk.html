Walk the wire

Ctrl+[ jumps to a formula’s source, Ctrl+] back — a wrong number is a trail, not a mystery.
The deck figure is wrong, and the VP’s note says which input to blame — three links upstream. Do not hunt by eye: Ctrl+[ jumps to a formula’s first precedent, hop by hop, until you’re standing on the source. Fix it there, and Ctrl+] rides the wire back down while everything reprices. Auditors call it tracing; the desk calls it Tuesday.

Step by step
  1. start on the deck figure H2 · ctrl+[ — you land on the subtotal it reads
  2. ctrl+[ again — now you’re at the top of the source block; the note says Software should read 290
  3. arrow to it, type 290 ↵ — the subtotal and the deck reprice on the spot
  4. ctrl+] ctrl+] — ride the wire back down and watch the deck figure land right
