Run the 13-week

Thirteen columns, one pattern — receipts − disbursements, filled right, rolls cash forward.
The RX desk staple: the front eight weeks of the 13-week cash flow. Receipts and disbursements are in from treasury — you build the spine: net flow each week, the beginning/ending roll-forward, the cushion against minimum liquidity (anchored — it does NOT walk), and totals on the FLOWS only. Stocks don’t sum: an ending balance total is how you spot a tourist.

The shortcuts in this drill
Alt walks: tap Alt, release, then the letters in sequence — each key steps one ribbon menu. On a Mac, tap ⌥ and use the same letters (KeyTips, Excel 2024+).
Ctrl+R⌘R on macFill right from the cell to the left
Same as fill down, but across — one quarter’s formula becomes the whole year in two keystrokes.
Ctrl+D⌘D on macFill down from the cell above
Build the logic once in the top cell, select down, and stamp it — the standard way to fill a schedule.
Ctrl+B⌘B on macBold
Bold marks structure: titles, total rows, anything the reviewer’s eye should land on first.
Alt h b t⌥ h b t on macTop border
Top border above a total row — the accounting ruling that says “this line sums the ones above.”
