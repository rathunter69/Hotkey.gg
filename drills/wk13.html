Run the 13-week

Thirteen columns, one pattern — receipts − disbursements, filled right, rolls cash forward.
The RX desk staple: the front eight weeks of the 13-week cash flow. Receipts and disbursements are in from treasury — you build the spine: net flow each week, the beginning/ending corkscrew, the cushion against minimum liquidity (anchored — it does NOT walk), and totals on the FLOWS only. Stocks don’t sum: an ending balance total is how you spot a tourist.

The keys
B6 =B4-B5 fill → · B8 =B7+B6 fill → · corkscrew C7 =B8 fill → · cushion B10 =B8-$B$12 fill → · totals J4 =SUM(B4:I4) + fill J4:J6 ↓ · dress B8:I8: ctrl+b + alt h b t
Step by step
  1. net flow first: B6 · =B4-B5 — receipts less disbursements · fill → to week 8
  2. ending cash: B8 · =B7+B6 · fill → · then the corkscrew: C7 · =B8 — next week opens where this one closed · fill →
  3. the cushion: B10 · =B8-$B$12 — minimum liquidity is ANCHORED (F4 locks it) · fill → · negative weeks are the whole point of this sheet
  4. totals on FLOWS only: J4 · =SUM(B4:I4) · select J4:J6 · ctrl+d — receipts, disbursements, net. Ending cash gets NO total
  5. dress the answer row: B8:I8 · ctrl+b · alt h b t
